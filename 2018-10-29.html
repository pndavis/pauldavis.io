Mid Quarter Update

Posted by Paul on October 29, 2018

Greetings from Seattle!

It's been about six weeks since my arrival in Seattle, and a lot has happened. I've been blessed to have a wonderful roommate that has been great in helping me adjust to the area. I have a new church home at Green Lake Presbyterian. And I just bought my first car, which has been incredibly helpful for ministry.

It's been a privilege over the last few weeks to get to know the leaders and students involved in RUF, but also to be able to reach out and meet some of the students new to the University of Washington. We have a really active and engaged freshman class this year, students that are really wanting to get connected. It has been wonderful to see students getting more and more connected to each other through bible studies, large group, or other events.

The University of Washington runs on a quarter system, and we are five weeks into a ten weeks quarter, so students are in the midst of projects and midterms. So while it feels like I have just started, the end of the quarter is rapidly ending. There is a lot to look forward to the next few weeks, and I'm so excited to see the ways in which God continues to work.

Our Ministry Team retreat. We spent a weekend away planning on how to best reach new students. The first week of school we played yard games in the Quad and handed out free popsicles. Our first Large Group. We brought in free food trucks for students afterwards. We went to a cornmaze an hour outside of Seattle. 40 students came in total, many freshmen!

Prayer Requests:

  • Pray for the students in the midst of their business. The quarter is only 10 weeks long, so they have many tests and midterms.
  • Pray the fall retreat goes well and RUF becomes a cohesive community
  • Pray for me to continue to adjust to the city, the campus, and to work as an intern. Getting a couple weeks into the quarter has helped me establish a good schedule.
  • Pray for me that I would be able to find friends and get more connected to my local church.
